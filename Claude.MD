# Capital Finance Club - Project Context

## Project Overview

The Capital Finance Club is a student-led finance organization at Knox Grammar School in Sydney, Australia. The club aims to provide high school students with practical exposure to quantitative finance, investment analysis, and financial markets through structured competitions, educational content, and industry connections.

## Leadership & Team

**Founder & President**: Ray
- Year 11 student at Knox Grammar School
- Target universities: Columbia, Wharton, MIT, Stanford (Fall 2027)
- Career aspiration: Quantitative finance
- Established high-level finance industry connections (PIMCO, KKR, Lipman Burgeon Partners)

## Club Mission & Philosophy

The Capital Finance Club focuses on developing genuine expertise in quantitative finance rather than superficial resume-building activities. Core principles include:

- Practical application of financial concepts through hands-on competitions
- Emphasis on quantitative rigor and systematic approaches
- Building real analytical skills that translate to institutional finance
- Creating opportunities for sophisticated financial analysis at the high school level

## Key Initiatives

### Knox Capital Challenge

The flagship competition designed and run by the Capital Finance Club. Details:
- Student investment competition
- Focus on fundamental analysis and quantitative reasoning
- Designed to mirror real-world institutional investment processes
- Structured to teach participants about portfolio construction and risk management

### Educational Programming

- Workshops on quantitative finance concepts
- Guest speakers from the finance industry
- Training sessions on financial modeling and analysis
- Resources for students interested in finance careers

## Related Projects & Context

### Occam's Hedge Research

Ray's primary research project exploring fundamental problems in modern hedging models:
- Focus on information efficiency and regime stability in deep hedging
- Key concepts: contextual overfitting, regime leakage
- Argues that deep hedging models fail due to being too capable rather than underpowered
- Potential positioning angle for university applications
